## 4. What changes when you switch — 4 cards

**Promise**: Each card names one specific automation that exists today and the manual work it replaces.

**Builds on**: §3 named the pain; this section names the mechanism.

**CTA**: One quiet "See it in the demo →" link beneath the grid.

**Visual**: 4 cards in a 2×2 grid on desktop, single column on mobile. Each with a small icon and the same shape: bold one-line headline → 2-sentence body → tiny proof line in mono font.

### Variant set (a) — *direct/punchy*

**Card 1 — Rent reconciliation runs in the same second as the payment.**
Mark an invoice paid; the income entry writes itself; the owner statement is current. Zero double-entry between bookkeeping and reporting.
*Replaces: end-of-month copy-paste between spreadsheet tabs.*

**Card 2 — Owners approve repairs from their email, not your phone.**
Send a magic-link approval request; the owner clicks Approve or Reject from email; the case advances stages on its own. The waiting clock pauses while it's with them.
*Replaces: WhatsApp screenshots and three days of follow-up.*

**Card 3 — Deadlines chase themselves.**
A cron checks every expiring lease, certificate, and policy every morning at 07:00 UTC. 30 days out you get an email; 7 days out it escalates to URGENT in your inbox.
*Replaces: hoping you remembered to remember.*

**Card 4 — Cases that go quiet come back to the top.**
Every workflow stage has an SLA budget. If a case stalls past it, the system surfaces it in your inbox automatically — and pauses the clock when you're waiting on a vendor or owner.
*Replaces: the weekly "what was I doing about that?" review.*

### Variant set (b) — *consultative/calm*

**Card 1 — Bookkeeping stays current without anyone doing the books.**
When a rent invoice is marked paid, the matching income entry is created in the same database transaction. Owner statements reflect that payment the moment they're opened.
*This is the part that used to take Friday afternoon.*

**Card 2 — Sign-off doesn't need anyone to log in.**
Approval requests go to the owner's email with a tokenised link. They Approve or Reject in two clicks; the audit log records who, when, and on which case. You stop being the carrier pigeon.
*Public link, 72-hour expiry, single-use, dispute path included.*

**Card 3 — Expiries surface themselves before they become problems.**
Daily checks for lease ends, insurance renewals, and compliance certificates. The same items appear in your inbox until they're resolved, and the cron de-duplicates so you only get one email per threshold.
*Two warning levels: 30 days out, then 7 days out.*

**Card 4 — Stuck cases stop being invisible.**
Each case stage carries its own SLA. Stage stalls past budget → case re-enters the inbox at URGENT severity. The clock automatically pauses when waitingOn is the owner, tenant, or vendor — not when it's you.
*Pause logic prevents false alarms.*

### Variant set (c) — *bold/contrarian*

**Card 1 — Stop reconciling. The platform already did it.**
The link between a paid invoice and a posted income entry is enforced at the database level, not as a "best effort" sync. There is no scenario where the books and the statement disagree.
*Owner statements are always up to date. No manual refresh needed.*

**Card 2 — Owners don't log in. They click.**
Every approval is a one-click decision delivered to the address you already have. Approve / reject / dispute — all recorded on the case timeline, all auditable, no portal training required.
*The owner never sees your dashboard. They don't need to.*

**Card 3 — Calendar reminders aren't a system. This is.**
Expiring leases, insurance, compliance certificates — checked every morning. Escalating severity. Auto-clearing the moment the underlying record changes. The opposite of a sticky note.
*15 hint types and counting.*

**Card 4 — The platform tells you what to do today.**
Cases stuck past their stage SLA. Approvals pending more than 24 hours. Vacant units past 30 days. Negative cashflow forecasts in the next 3 months. All of it surfaced — automatically — before you have to ask.
*Suggested actions are one click away.*

## 6. How it works in your week — weekly rhythm

**Promise**: Reframe from onboarding to operating. "This is what Mondays look like" not "Step 1: import your data."

**Builds on**: §5 showed a single day; this section connects the days.

**CTA**: None inside the section.

**Visual**: Three horizontal panels (or vertical stack on mobile). Each with a small day-of-week indicator above it.

### Variant set (a) — *direct/punchy*

**Monday morning — Open the inbox.**
Whatever happened over the weekend is already triaged. Overdue invoices, urgent maintenance from the portal, approvals back from owners. Most rows have a one-click action. The rest take a phone call.

**Mid-week — Touch a case only when it's waiting on you.**
Vendors assigned → cases auto-advance. Approvals granted → cases auto-advance. Invoices paid → cases auto-advance. The cases that stall past their SLA come back to the top of the inbox.

**Friday afternoon — Don't write owner statements. They wrote themselves.**
Open each property report and forward the link. Numbers are current because every rent payment updated the underlying ledger in real time.

### Variant set (b) — *consultative/calm*

**Monday — A short list, not a deluge.**
The cron ran at 07:00 and filed everything into your inbox by severity. Last week's loose ends carry over with their stage SLAs visibly counting down. You handle three things before your first coffee.

**Tuesday through Thursday — Cases move themselves.**
Most of the work is conversations with tenants and vendors. The system handles the bookkeeping: vendor invoice attached → expense entry pre-filled; approval granted → case advances; status flipped to Completed → owner statement reflects the cost.

**Friday — Statements are sent, not drafted.**
There is no copy-paste step. Open the property report (PDF), check it looks right, forward it. The bank reconciliation step you used to do? It happened in the same transaction as the invoice flip.

### Variant set (c) — *bold/contrarian*

**Monday is not a catch-up day anymore.**
Because there's nothing to catch up on. Reminders fired, cases advanced, approvals were collected, deadlines were tracked — all while you were off. The inbox shows you only what survived the automation.

**The middle of the week is conversation, not data entry.**
Every email you write is logged on the case. Every comment is timestamped. Every status change has an audit trail. Nothing gets re-typed into a sheet.

**Friday afternoon is shorter than it used to be.**
Owner statements are current. Compliance items are tracked. Cashflow forecasts for the next 3, 6, and 12 months are already computed. You forward links. You go home.
