00 — Introduction

What is GroundWorkPM?

GroundWorkPM is a professional property management platform built for modern property managers. It consolidates income tracking, expense management, tenant administration, maintenance, and owner reporting into a single, opinionated tool.

The GroundWorkPM landing page — your starting point before signing up

The platform is built around four core workflows:

  • Financial tracking — Log income and expenses per property and unit, with automatic P&L calculations
  • Tenant management — Full tenant lifecycle from onboarding through lease renewal and vacating
  • Maintenance operations — Kanban-style job tracking with vendor assignment and cost logging
  • Owner reporting — Professional PDF statements with itemised income, expenses, and net returns
💡
Try a demo first When you sign up, you can load a pre-seeded demo property (Al Seef Residences — 20 units in Bahrain, or Sandton Heights — 9 units in South Africa) so you can explore the platform with real data before entering your own.
01 — User Roles

Roles & Access Control

GroundWorkPM has four roles. Access is enforced at both the route and API level — users cannot reach pages outside their role.

Role Who it's for What they can access
MANAGER / ADMIN Property manager, agency administrator Full access — dashboard, tenants, income, expenses, maintenance, reports, settings, billing
ACCOUNTANT Bookkeeper, accounts staff Financial pages only — income, expenses, invoices, arrears, forecast, reports. No settings or user management.
OWNER Property owner / landlord The Report page only — owner statements with income breakdown, deductions, and net returns. Cannot see financial details.
TENANT PORTAL Tenant (no account needed) Public, token-based portal — view invoices, download PDFs, view documents, submit maintenance requests.
ℹ️
Org-Admin vs Super-Admin Both use role ADMIN. The difference is organizationId: org-admins have an org ID and are scoped to their organisation; super-admins have no org ID and see all organisations via the platform admin panel.
02 — Getting Started

Sign Up & Onboarding

New users are guided through a 3-step onboarding wizard immediately after signing up. No data entry is required to explore the platform — you can load a demo property in Step 3.

Login — email/password or Google OAuth
Signup — 30-day free trial, no card required

Onboarding: 3 Steps

1

Set up your first property

Enter the property name, type (Long-term rental or Short-let / Airbnb), currency, address, and city. If you signed up via Google OAuth, you'll also name your organisation here. Click Continue →.

2

Add your units

Add each unit inside the property — unit number (e.g. "A1", "101"), type (1 Bed, 2 Bed, Penthouse…), and monthly rent. Click Add another unit to keep going, or Skip for now to add units later from the dashboard.

3

Done — load sample data (optional)

Click Go to Dashboard → to start with your empty property, or pick a demo from the card picker and click Load sample property →. Demo properties come with 3 months of tenants, income, expenses, and arrears pre-loaded.

💡
Forgot your password? Use the Forgot password? link on the login page. A reset link valid for 1 hour is sent to your email via Resend. Enter your new password on the reset page and you'll be redirected back to login.

Attention Required Alerts

Four alert cards show the health of your portfolio. Each card is colour-coded: red = critical  amber = warning  green = clear.

  • Lease Watch — tenants with leases expiring in ≤30 days or already expired
  • Unpaid Rent — invoices that are overdue or unsent this month
  • Maintenance — open maintenance jobs, overdue recurring schedules
  • Arrears — tenants with outstanding balances older than 30 days

Month Picker

The month picker (top right of the dashboard header) controls which month's data is shown. Click the left/right arrows to move months or click the month label to jump to a specific date. A Back to current link appears when you're viewing a past month.

07 — Expenses & Petty Cash

Expenses & Petty Cash

Log every property expense with category, scope, vendor, tax, and payment method. Expenses are separated between operating costs (P&L) and capital/sunk costs (balance sheet).

Expenses — filtered monthly view with category breakdown and KPI strip

Expense Scope

ScopeAssigned toUse case
UNITSpecific unitRepair inside a single unit, appliance replacement
PROPERTYWhole propertyBuilding insurance, common-area maintenance, landscaping
PORTFOLIONo specific propertySoftware subscriptions, management office costs

Sunk Costs vs Operating Costs

Mark an expense as a Sunk Cost / Capital Item using the checkbox when adding or editing. Sunk costs are shown in reports as "Capital Items" and are excluded from the P&L — they don't reduce net profit but remain visible for reference.

Petty Cash

Petty Cash — running balance with topup and withdrawal entries

The petty cash balance is always recalculated from all entries (never stored as a number). Add Topup entries when you add money to the float, and select Petty Cash as the payment method when logging an expense to deduct it automatically.

⚠️
Tax on expenses Line items within an expense can have tax applied. Tax type ADDITIVE VAT adds tax on top of the net amount. WITHHELD deducts tax from the gross (e.g. withholding tax on contractor payments). Both types are reported separately in the Tax Summary report.

Adding a Maintenance Job

Click Add Job

P&L Formula

Gross Income = All income entries excl. DEPOSIT type
− Agent Commissions (income entries linked to agents)
− Operating Expenses (expenses where isSunkCost = false)
= Net Profit

Capital/sunk cost expenses are shown separately as "Capital Items" and do not affect net profit.

Sending a Report to the Owner

10 — Operations

Operations

Supporting modules that keep your portfolio compliant, insured, and organised — forecast, vendors, insurance, compliance, assets, and calendar.

Cash Flow Forecast — 3/6/12 month projections
Vendors — contractor and supplier directory
Insurance — policy tracking with expiry alerts
Compliance — safety certificate status dashboard
Asset Register — inventory with maintenance schedules
Calendar — upcoming lease renewals, maintenance, and payments

Forecast

Projects income and expenses for the next 3, 6, or 12 months using your recurring expenses, insurance premiums, lease renewal dates, and management agreements. Uses buildForecast() — no AI, pure deterministic projection from your existing data.

Vendors

Maintain a directory of contractors and suppliers. Each vendor has a category (Contractor, Supplier, Utility Provider, Consultant, Other), contact details, KRA PIN, and bank details. Vendors can be linked to expenses, maintenance jobs, assets, and recurring expenses — use the Vendor Select dropdown whenever you see a contractor/supplier field.

ℹ️
Deactivating vendors You cannot delete a vendor with linked records. Instead, use the Deactivate toggle — the vendor is hidden from new selections but all historical links remain intact.

Insurance

Track policies per property with type (Building, Public Liability, Contents, Other), insurer, premium, frequency, coverage amount, and broker. Policies expiring within 30 days trigger automated alerts. Documents can be uploaded directly to each policy record.

Compliance & Certificates

Track compliance certificates (Fire Safety, Lift Inspection, Electrical, Gas Safe, etc.) with expiry dates. Status is computed automatically: EXPIRED  EXPIRING SOON (≤30 days)  VALID  ONGOING (no expiry). Daily cron notifications alert managers when certificates are due.

Asset Register

Maintain an inventory of property assets — appliances, fixtures, furniture, plant & equipment. Each asset can have recurring maintenance schedules and a maintenance log (which can be linked to expense entries). The register tracks serial numbers, warranty dates, and replacement values.

Tenant Portal — invoice history, outstanding balance, documents, and maintenance request form

Mobile Navigation

On mobile, the sidebar is replaced by a fixed bottom navigation bar:

  • Primary tabs: Home (Dashboard), Tenants, Income, Airbnb — always visible
  • More menu: Tap the ⋮ icon to open a full-page drawer with all remaining navigation sections (Finances, Operations, Settings)
  • Table views switch to stacked card layouts automatically on small screens

Automated Notifications

A daily cron job (07:00 UTC) sends email alerts to all Managers and Admins with property access when:

TriggerAlert typeWindow
Lease expiringLEASE_EXPIRY_30D / LEASE_EXPIRY_7D≤30 days or ≤7 days
Invoice overdueINVOICE_OVERDUE>7 days past due date
Compliance cert expiringCERT_EXPIRY≤30 days or ≤7 days
Insurance policy endingINSURANCE_EXPIRY≤30 days or ≤7 days
Urgent maintenance openMAINTENANCE_URGENTStill OPEN after 4+ hours

Notifications are deduplicated — you will not receive the same alert repeatedly within the dedup window.

Multi-Organisation Support

Users can belong to multiple organisations. After login, if you have multiple memberships and no active org is set, you are redirected to /select-org to pick your organisation. You can also switch organisations at any time from the org switcher in the sidebar header without logging out.

Data Import

Navigate to Settings → Import to bulk-import data from Excel/CSV. Download the template file from the import page, fill it in, and upload. Supported import types: income, expenses, tenants, and properties. Imports are validated and applied as upserts — re-importing with the same identifiers updates existing records rather than duplicating them.
